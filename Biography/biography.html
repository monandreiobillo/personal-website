Still Developing

A Biography by Mon Andrei Obillo

Table of Contents:

  1. Childhood
  2. Teenage Years
  3. College Years

Childhood

My childhood story isn't one for the books.1 There isn't much to tell about it.2 For one, the most interesting part of my childhood would be that I graduated kinder as a valedictorian.3 It was great, and I did a whole speech in front of so many people, but it wasn't as remarkable as people may think.4 I was kinder, after all, and what I achieved then is incomparable with the achievements of other people in the levels that actually matter, such as right now in college.5 I was just a quiet kid then.6 I didn't have so many friends growing up, and those few friends I had are so distant now.7 I didn't have that iPad kid era during my childhood, due to the fact that I didn't have access to gadgets early in life.8 I had my first tablet at around 8 or 9, and even then I had restrictions, which did help to avoid the addiction towards gadgets that early in life.9 I studied at a catholic school, which meant constant prayers before each class, and having to go through monthly masses and angelus every noon.10 Back then, I wasn't allowed to have my own money, and had to bring lunch everyday to school, which made me jealous of my classmates who could buy at the canteen.11 I did eventually get the freedom to buy using my own allowance, sometime around Grade 5 onwards.12 I wasn't fond of pop culture, mainly due to not having access to the internet and not being exposed to current trends.13 Until now I'm still not a fan of the latest... everything.14 I remember vividly that I auditioned for the school church choir and when asked to sing anything I didn't know any popular songs so I had to sing a church song, which isn't bad considering I was auditioning for a church choir, but in that moment I felt a bit ashamed for not knowing what others could easily sing at that moment.15 Looking back, it's easy to understand why I wasn't the most social as a kid.16 I wasn't able to keep up with others, and that led to them not being fond of me.17 It's mainly the reason why I was quiet and didn't have much of that exciting and fun childhood everyone brags of.18 Even when I tried to open up, my words felt too small next to everyone else's stories.19 Still, those quieter days taught me to observe and appreciate the little routines around me.20

Teenage Years

Half of my teenage years were spent locked up in my own home, as the pandemic forced the world to shut down.1 A lot changed during that time, and as time progressed, I became somehow unrecognizable compared to before the pandemic.2 Not in a physical sense, although with puberty happening during that time, I was changing and growing out of my younger self.3 Before the pandemic, I was so outdated with almost everything, and all of a sudden, the need to be chronically online due to school opened me up to more of the world than I knew before.4 Before I knew it… I still wasn't fond of the latest trends.5 Instead, I found a liking to a lot of fiction, whether it was books or movies, I had so much time on my hands to consume fictional materials.6 I found a liking to writing fictional stories as well, drastically improving my vocabulary and capability to words.7 It may not be as obvious now since its been years since I last wrote.8 I also took a liking with designing and doing creative digital designs, mainly to support my writing journey by making cover art for my works, which I published on a platform called "Wattpad", but evolved to making general designs, mainly for school purposes.9 Due to being chronically online, I became a bit obnoxious with chats and messages online.10 Those few friends I had were quite surprised when I became noisy online, messaging more than 5 messages for just one meaning.11 I could be asking them about an assignment, but instead of sending one message containing all my thoughts, it would be dispersed into more than 7 messages.12 It did cause some people to be irritated and slowly drift away from me.13 I guess it was mostly due to the isolation, and that despite my almost "antisocial" behavior in the past, I still craved interactions with others.14 That and the fact that I never learned proper communication and socialization skills growing up.15 My mental health wasn't the best, and by the time the pandemic was over, I couldn't transition easily.16 Being stuck at home for years with no way of going out and interacting with others caused a severe impact on me.17 It took months to finally adjust to the new normal.18 Being under lockdown practically my whole teenage life meant the changes and the new unlocked knowledge and hobbies weren't honed in the outside world and to the social world.19 I developed and grew without the factors like going out with friends and developing my first relationships (if I was ever lucky), and so after the pandemic was a whole other development stage.20 A lot of the things I should've learned and developed in me happened too late in my adolescent years, and even as a new adult I'm still developing.21

College Years

Now, in college came a lot of new experiences.1 I consider my senior high school a part of my journey in this phase because it happened after the pandemic and after I adjusted to the new normal.2 Interestingly, I was able to travel a lot more after the pandemic, with my family going to boracay once and even the small travels for school projects and such.3 There was a hint of anxiety trying new things, like riding the jeepney, which was uncommon for me because as a kid I didn't have to go alone, and as a teen I was mostly home.4 The first time I went home at night was during my senior high school as well, with 7:30PM class dismissals and the eventual 9PM schedule due to my commitment to UE Chorale, which until now I still am a part of.5 It was during this time that I had my first real group of friends, who may have been a bit too influential to me, having me try new things (I regret, like trying alcohol when I turned 18).6 It wasn't the best, but I learned so much from just those two years of senior high school.7 College is a different story.8 The experience was so different from senior high, as my blockmates and friends from the class were more mature and reserved.9 I barely see any drama that was so common during the senior high phase.10 It was at this time that I felt like I had full control over my own life, with the course that I love and the scholarships to keep me going (DOST and from UE Chorale).11 I felt that life was more real, and that things will keep on becoming more “real”, as some choices do lead to so much more.12 I found love during this time, and with just a simple interaction, we became more than I ever dreamed of in a partner.13 I wasn't exactly waiting or trying to find someone, rather it just happened.14 And even after a year and counting, we're still together and still staying strong.15 It was a decision that showed me that life is “real” and the things I choose change the course of my life, sometimes for the better, as with my partner.16 My world grew from just myself to an ever-expanding possibility, and it pushed me to be more than what I used to be.17 I may not have had a “fun” childhood or that “YOLO” teenage years but I certainly crossed a lot of things in the College Journey Checklist everyone claims to be “canon college events” that everyone seems to get.18 I'm able to find better and more sustainable friendships, get a healthy relationship, and have a stable journey towards the next parts of my life with the career I love.19 It may not have been the best past, but one thing I learned from all of this is that I can change the future and make it better.20
