I Can't Let Go of Minecraft

Mon Andrei Obillo

Personal

Minecraft

Gaming

Childhood

Memories

Call me childish, but I'm still an avid minecraft player. I still find time to play and enjoy my favorite game. Does it mess with my focus and waste my time? Yes, but it's often worth it considering I improve everytime I play.

Ever since...

I have always dreamed of having my own copy of Minecraft. As a kid, I often heard so much stories from classmates taking about how fun playing minecraft was. Tignan mo tong gawa ko na bahay oh (Look at the house I made). a classmate once said. Of course, jelousy sparked in me and I knew that I really wanted to get a copy. And so I asked my parents to get me the game. I didn't have a PC or laptop then, and only had a tablet. So I had to ask them for the then-called Minecraft: Pocket Edition so that I can play on my tablet. They did get me the game, but its through connections, so to speak. My mom was part of the tech sales industry and had someone download on my tablet a version of the game that works on the current Android OS version that my tablet had, which was an older version that had just a limited world size generation. I was really thankful, yes. But I yearned for that infinite terrain that newer versions provided. And because it is just downloaded externally, I couldn't really update the game to a newer and better version.

Being Desparate

I eventually tried out alternatives. There was a game similar to Minecraft called SurvivalCraft, which was this moody and shader-packed alternative to Minecraft. It did have a 20-minute playing limit and had about a maximum of 10 worlds (i think) that I can make. It was, of course, because of a paywall. It did seem better because it had features that Minecraft didn't have, such as a better Electrical system and overall just a better experience. But again, since I had no money to even buy an official and non-restricted copy, I was forced to just endure the sorrow of not having any way to play my favorite sandbox game. I also had other means, like using ShareIt, back when it was still usable and not filled with ads, to borrow a copy of Minecraft from my cousins and even friends from school. It did work, and much better than all previous options, but it came at a cost, that being I can't sign in and join servers. It wasn't much of a bummer, and I had a lot of fun with the ever-expansive world it had, but I eventually lost interest. I let go of the game and the desire completely because there wasn't much left to do.

A huge leap in my dream

When the pandemic struck, I was bored beyond words. There was absolutely nothing worth doing because I just had no motivation to even do my own school works. Halfway through the lockdown, I had the opportunity to buy an official copy of the game, albeit it was through questionable methods (Like secretly stashing up coins and spare money until I could afford the game). I know it was wrong, considering the money came from my parents and it was often change for everytime I was asked to buy something, often telling them there was no money left. Once I had my own official copy, I became instantly addicted again. What used to be just pirated copies of the game now became this fun and convenient official copy that updates regularly. Plus, I get to join servers (yay!) which is an entirely different experience from just survival and creative mode that usually comes from the game.

Well, finally

When I gained access to servers and having the means to make my own Xbox and Minecraft account (it's actually one and the same), I became hooked onto playing minigames and socializing. I remember the first minigame I ever played was actually in this server called The Hive, which is Survival Games (plays on the concept of Hunger Games). In the next few months years, I became really into playing various minigames and trying out various servers to get an entirely new experience each time. I did have times when I didn't play, or stopped completely, mostly due to school and schedule reasons.

I just once dreamed of it

Even now, in the middle of my college journey, I still do play minecraft. I do so every now and then, and I'm still fond of it. I'm quite proud, albeit with a bit of regret, that I achieved what my younger self just dreamed of and barely got to do so. Minecraft is a huge part of who I am, representing a part of me that just wanted to experience the magic and the fun of building and taking on adventures. I can't just let go of that part of me, so call me childish, but I'm still an avid minecraft player.
